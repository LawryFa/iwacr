At Integration of Women and Children Refugees, we strive to create a nurturing community that fosters empowerment, resilience, and lasting integration.

  • Support

    Our objective is to offer a wide range of services, including education, healthcare, legal assistance, and counseling, to meet the holistic needs of women and children refugees.

  • Social Intergation

    Foster social integration: We aim to facilitate the integration of women and children refugees into their new communities by promoting cultural understanding, social connections, and a sense of belonging.

  • Training

    Empower through education and skills training: Our objective is to equip refugee women and children with the necessary knowledge, skills, and opportunities to enhance their self-reliance and contribute positively to society.

  • Rights and Wellbeing

    Advocate for the rights and well-being of refugees: We strive to be a voice for refugee women and children, advocating for their rights, protection, and access to essential services, while raising awareness about their unique challenges and contributions.

What We Do

Women and children are the most vulnerable migrants-here's a few of the challenges they face

  • Gender-based violence

    Women and girls are particularly vulnerable to gender-based violence, including sexual harassment, assault, and exploitation. They may face risks both during their journey and within the refugee camps.

    Read More

  • Exploitation and Traficking

    Women and children refugees are at a higher risk of being trafficked or exploited due to their vulnerable situation. They may be forced into labor, subjected to sexual exploitation, or coerced into illegal activities.

    Read More

  • Lack of access to Healthcare

    Women and children often face limited access to essential healthcare services, including reproductive health care, prenatal and postnatal care, and mental health support. This can lead to increased risks during pregnancy and childbirth, as well as long-term health consequences.

    Read More

  • Educational barriers

    Refugee children, especially girls, often face significant barriers to accessing education. Lack of schools, language barriers, discrimination, and limited resources can prevent them from receiving a quality education, limiting their future opportunities.

    Read More
